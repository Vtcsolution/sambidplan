# PHASE 2 — Match Scoring & Distribution

---

## LinkedIn Post — Phase 2

**TITLE / HOOK:**
I gave every user the same 50,000 contracts. Nobody used it.

---

Early version of Sambid Notify was simple.
Fetch contracts. Show them to everyone.

We had 50,000 opportunities in the database.
Engagement was close to zero.

A roofing company doesn't need IT contracts.
A cybersecurity firm doesn't need janitorial bids.

Showing everything to everyone isn't a product.
It's a data dump.

So I built a scoring engine.

Every contract gets scored 0–100 per user:
— NAICS industry match → 50 points
— Business certifications → 20 points
— Deadline urgency → 20 points
— Contract value range → 10 points

Only the top scored contracts reach your feed.
Different business = completely different results.

After this one change, user engagement went up immediately.

The data didn't change.
The delivery did.

Most products already have the right data.
The problem is always the delivery.

## Skool Post — Phase 2

**TITLE:**
We Had 50,000 Contracts. Nobody Opened Them. Here's Why.

---

When we launched, our logic was simple.

More data = more value. Right?

Wrong.

We had 50,000 federal contracts available.
Users logged in, saw a wall of irrelevant opportunities, and never came back.

The problem wasn't the volume.
It was that we were treating every business the same.

So we built a match scoring system.

Every contract gets a score from 0 to 100 for each user specifically:
— Does it match their industry? +50 points
— Does it match their business certifications? +20 points
— Is the deadline approaching? +20 points
— Is the contract value realistic for their size? +10 points

Only the highest scored contracts get delivered.

Result: users now open their feed and see 3–10 contracts that actually match their business. Not 50,000 that don't.

Engagement went up without changing a single piece of data.

---

Here's what I want you to think about:

How much of what you're delivering to your users actually matters to them?

Or are you sending everything and hoping they figure out what's relevant?

The product that filters first always wins over the product that shows more.

Drop a comment — what does your current "data dump" problem look like?

# PHASE 4 — AI Tools

---

## LinkedIn Post — Phase 4

**TITLE / HOOK:**
Small businesses were paying $15,000 for one federal proposal. I put the same capability in a button.

---

Federal contracting is not simple.

To respond to one government opportunity, a small business needs:

— A capability statement proving they qualify
— A full technical proposal (often 20+ pages)
— An RFP analysis to understand what the agency actually wants
— Competitive market research
— A go/no-go decision before they invest days of work

Each one takes hours. Or thousands of dollars to outsource.

Most small businesses give up before they start.
Not because they're not qualified.
Because the process is designed for people with lawyers and consultants.

So I embedded AI directly into Sambid Notify.

Every user now has access to:
— Proposal Builder → full draft generated from one click
— RFP Analyzer → plain language breakdown of exactly what's being asked
— Capability Statement → professional document built from their profile
— AI Market Research → competitive intelligence for their industry
— Go/No-Go Scorer → should you even bid on this?

The knowledge didn't change.
The access did.

A small business owner with zero federal experience now competes like a 10-year veteran.

Not because they learned faster.
Because the right tool was already in their hands.

# PHASE 5 — Payments, Plans & Commission System

---

## LinkedIn Post — Phase 5

**TITLE / HOOK:**
I built a sales network without hiring a single salesperson.

---

Sales is the bottleneck most founders don't solve early enough.

You build a great product.
Then you become the only person selling it.

That's a ceiling. Not a growth strategy.

So I built a commission system directly into Sambid Notify.

Anyone can become a support member:
— They share a unique referral code
— Someone signs up through that code and pays
— 15% commission credited to their account instantly
— After 100 Pro/Enterprise referrals → 7.5% on every recurring payment, forever

No invoices. No spreadsheets. No disputes.
Commission calculated the moment a payment clears.
Withdrawal requested from their own dashboard.
Admin reviews and pays. Done.

The result:

People are selling Sambid Notify every day.
People I've never met.
In cities I've never visited.
To businesses I would never have reached alone.

And I've never manually written a commission check.

Revenue grew. The team didn't have to.

Most founders think sales requires salespeople.
It requires a system that makes selling worth it for everyone involved.
